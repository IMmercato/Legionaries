Quaestiones

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need to know Latin or Roman history?

Not at all. You need curiosity and a willingness to connect modern building to old problems. A Roman aqueduct is just a gravity-fed pipeline — build your version of it.

Can I work in a team?

Yes, teams of up to 3. Gladiators split equally between members (rounded down), with bonus units going to the shared territory pool. So a 5-Gladiator team of 3 gets 1 each + 2 to the pool.

What tech stack can I use?

Anything. Web, hardware, embedded, physical builds, simulations. The only requirement is that it's functional, documented, and you can demonstrate it working.

How are Gladiators scored?

Reviewers evaluate: functionality (does it work?), depth (did you go beyond surface-level?), connection to antiquity (is the ancient inspiration meaningful?), and documentation quality.

What happens when territories collide?

The larger army wins. In a tie, the earlier submission keeps the tile. Losers don't lose their gladiators — only the contested tile changes hands.

Is there a limit on submissions?

No hard limit. But reviewers will notice if you're farming shallow projects. Quality over quantity — one well-crafted submission beats five half-hearted ones every time.

How long does the program run?

Through summer 2026. You can submit multiple projects throughout the campaign. The map is a living record of the whole summer.

My idea is weird. Does it qualify?

Ask in the thread. The general rule: if a Roman engineer, architect, or general would recognize the underlying problem you're solving, you're probably good. Weird is encouraged.
